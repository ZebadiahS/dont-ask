Making Place

Making Place explores how graphic design can reveal and create place by applying architectural and geographical theory to design practice. Drawing on the work of theorists such as Yi-Fu Tuan, John Agnew, Henri Lefebvre, and Edward Soja, I examine place as an individual, personal experience shaped by the physical, imagined, and lived. Making Place proposes two categories: Revealing Place, where designers layer interpretation onto existing content or locations, and Creating Place, where designers originate both content and form. Through projects including chair pants, posters, and branded packaging, this thesis investigates how designers make visible the personal connections that shape our understanding of the world.
